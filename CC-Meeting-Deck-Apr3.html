PRODUCT OPERATIONS

Keeping 491 Products Current

11 brands. Prices change. Collections launch. SKUs get discontinued. Here's how updates flow from manufacturer to customer's screen.
The Update Flow
①
Manufacturer Sends Update
New price list PDF, new collection launch, discontinuation notice — arrives via email. Roger uploads it to Google Drive. Manual
↓
②
AI Parses + Compares
Dashboard AI reads the price list, compares against the Master Price List (SKU, MSRP, Dealer Cost, Status). Flags what changed: "BRIZO updated 23 prices. 5 increased, 2 new SKUs, 1 discontinued." Automated
↓
③
Roger Reviews + Approves
Dashboard shows a clear diff: old price vs. new, margin impact, new products to add. Roger approves or adjusts retail pricing. Manual decision
↓
④
Google Sheets Updates → Website Updates
Approved changes write to Google Sheets. Products tab and Master Price List sync. Website reflects new prices, new products, removed items — instantly, no developer needed. Automated
What the Dashboard Flags
!Price changes: Dealer cost went up but retail price hasn't been updated — your margin is shrinking
!Discontinued: TOTO stopped making this model — 2 active quotes reference it
+New products: California Faucets launched a new collection — 8 SKUs ready to add
↻New finishes: Kohler added Matte Black to 12 existing faucets — update variants
$Below MAP: 3 products priced below Minimum Advertised Price — compliance risk
The Master Price List already exists — SKU, MSRP, MAP, Dealer Cost, Margin % across all brands. It's the backbone. Manufacturer updates sync against it. Roger always knows his real margins.
One edit, everywhere at once. Change a price in Sheets → website updates, dashboard reflects it, active quotes get flagged if affected. No more "the website says one price but we quoted another."

FINANCE OPERATIONS

Following the Money

Money in from customers. Money out to manufacturers. The spread is your profit — and now you can actually see it.
Money In — What the Platform Tracks
$
Customer Payments via Stripe
Every invoice, every deposit, every final payment — logged automatically. Finance sees it the second it clears. No checking the bank and cross-referencing WhatsApp. Automated
$
Deposits + Partial Payments
Large projects get split: 50% deposit, 50% on delivery. Stripe tracks both. Pipeline shows balance remaining per deal. No mental math. Automated
$
Stripe → Bank Payouts
Stripe deposits to your Mexico bank account on schedule. Dashboard shows pending payouts, completed payouts, and payout history. Automated
Money Out — Manufacturer Payments
$
Pay Dealer Cost to Manufacturer
When Roger orders from Kohler or BRIZO, he pays the dealer cost. He logs the amount in the pipeline. The system calculates: customer paid $15,000 – dealer cost $9,200 = $5,800 gross profit. Manual — Roger logs cost
The Finance Dashboard
✓Revenue this month: Total collected via Stripe — real-time, not end-of-month surprise
✓Outstanding invoices: Who owes what, how overdue, one-click WhatsApp reminder
✓Pipeline value: Total deals in progress — quoted, paid, awaiting delivery
✓Real margins per deal: Sale price vs. dealer cost from Master Price List — not guesswork
✓Margins by brand: Which manufacturers are most profitable? Ruvati at 45%? BRIZO at 28%?
!Cash flow gap alerts: Customer deposit received but manufacturer payment due — how much runway do you have?
What Finance does manually: Verify bank deposits match Stripe payouts. Log manufacturer payments. Approve refunds. Handle tax reporting. Everything else — revenue tracking, invoice status, margin calculation, overdue alerts — is automatic.
The big unlock: Right now, nobody knows the real profit on a deal until weeks later. With dealer costs in the Master Price List and payments in Stripe, the dashboard shows margin the moment a deal closes. Roger knows which brands, which deal sizes, and which customer types are actually making money.
